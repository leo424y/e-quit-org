社區發展研究與實務應用工作坊
Workshop on Community Development Research and Practice

Friday, 2 Dec, 2011
Theme: Women’s Reproductive Health and Development
This session demonstrates how community development approach can be applied in an important public health issue, in particular, a women’s reproductive health project in rural China over a span of 8 years. The World Health Organization acknowledged its success and recommended its methodology for the developing countries for country development.

Saturday, 3 Dec, 2011
Theme: Uprooting Tobacco at Grassroots
Tobacco farmers are one of, if not the most, the vulnerable group along the chain of tobacco production. In most cases their livelihood solely depends on tobacco plantation. An innovative for-profit model of farmer cooperative has a high potential to help tobacco growers substitute their crop, and it involves a different way of organizing and training of farmers. Therefore, it is important for both researchers and advocates to be aware of the twin engine of tobacco education and crop substitution for tobacco control.
